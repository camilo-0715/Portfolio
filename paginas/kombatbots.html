kombat bots

The main idea was to build two robots controlled by two motors and a caster wheel, with collisions between them detected by sensors to decrease each robot's combat life. The robot that runs out of life first loses. It was meant to be simple and straightforward.

Typically, original battlebots have a more direct way of damaging the opponent than just collisions. Some are equipped with flamethrowers, electric sparks, giant hammers... Of course, equipping robots with flamethrowers wasn't an option, but the idea was to include a weapon. The primary method of inflicting damage would be direct contact, but the mechanical and electronic design of a weapon for each robot seemed intriguing. Each robot would have its own unique weapon to damage the opponent in a more specific way than mere collisions.

But there was also a desire for these robots to be unique and have a personal touch. That's when the inspiration struck: to base the robots on the popular characters from the game Mortal Kombat, Scorpion and Sub-Zero. These two characters are arch-enemies destined to fight to the death until one emerges victorious, perfectly aligning with the concept of the robots. Hence, the project was named 'Kombat-Bot' in reference to the game, with one robot representing Scorpion and the other Sub-Zero.
